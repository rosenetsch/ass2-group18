Meaningful Acknowledgement to Country

This feature helps event creators build respectful and meaningful acknowledgement statements while promoting reconciliation, cultural awareness, and respectful event practices.

Acknowledgement to Country

  • Can be spoken by anyone
  • Used at meetings and events
  • Shows respect to Traditional Custodians

Welcome to Country

  • Performed by Elders or Traditional Custodians
  • Formal welcome onto Country
  • May include ceremonies and performances

Acknowledgement Options

Option 1
No Acknowledgement

No acknowledgement statement appears in event details.

Option 2
Generic Acknowledgement

Displays a respectful generic acknowledgement statement.

Option 3
Enhanced Acknowledgement

Includes educational guidance and research support.

Common Mistakes and Ethical Considerations

  • Copying acknowledgements without research
  • Using incorrect Nation names
  • Confusing Welcome to Country with Acknowledgement to Country
  • Treating acknowledgements like a box-ticking activity
  • Using culturally inappropriate wording
